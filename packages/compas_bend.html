Bending-active package for the compas framework

Bending-active structural systems utilize large geometric deformations of structural elements to increase the overall stiffness of the structural system. Starting with initially straight members, highly curved stiff geometries can be realized with a minimum amount of material. The best known structural systems that utilize the bending-active principal are elastic gridshells, but bending-active elements have also been used to provide underlying support systems for membranes and knits.

The compas_bend package offers several options for the design and simulation of bending-active systems. The package provides the necessary datastructures and methods for manipulation and simulation of beam, cable, and bar based bending active systems, with the potential to expand to other element types, such as membranes.

Currently, the package focuses on dynamic relaxation as the primary forward simulation method, but will be extended to other physics-based methods.
