compas_ctrl
  • author: Andrew Liew
  • status: private
  • version: 0.1.0
https://github.com/compas-dev/compas_ctrl

Control package for the compas framework.

The construction of various structural forms can be sensitive to fabrication and erection tolerances, as well as the levels of any pre- or post-tensioning. Some structures that fall into this category include stressed cable-net and membrane structures, and bending active geometries such as strained grid-shells. As the shape and internal stress state of these structures is dependent on both the erection sequence and the target/realised geometry, it can be advantageous to allow for some degree of control during construction, so that the completed structure can match closely the initial design and analysis.

The compas_ctrl package lays out control algorithms and methods that can assimilate available fabrication and erection data, such as spatial and force measurement data, to dynamically control the geometry and stress-state of the structure. This process is different to progressing with construction in a linear manner and “seeing what you get” at the end. The algorithms have been successfully used on the construction of the 1:1 prototype of the NEST HiLo cable-net and fabric formwork concrete shell roof structure, instigated at the ITA within ETHZ, Switzerland.
